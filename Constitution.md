# Article I -- The Stony Brook Computing Society

The full name of this organization shall be the Stony Brook Computing
Society (hereafter referred to as the \'SBCS\'). The SBCS is registered
with the College of Engineering and Applied Sciences and the Department
of Student Engagement and Activities.\"

# Article II -- Statement of Purpose

## Section I

SBCS fosters a community for anyone interested in technology. We provide
academic,

professional, and social events so students can grow as both programmers
and people. Our industry connections, destressing games, and educational
workshops serve your success.

## Section II

1.  To help our members, and students at Stony Brook, better their
    > technology education both inside and outside the classroom.

2.  To facilitate our members' transition from school to the technology
    > industry, we are creating opportunities to learn about potential
    > careers, preparing students for interviews and career fairs, and
    > connecting students with potential jobs.

3.  We aim to encourage positive relationships among our students, with
    > their diverse backgrounds and interests, that will persist
    > throughout their college careers and afterward.

The purpose of the "Stony Brook Computing Society" is to foster a
community for anyone interested in technology. We provide academic,
professional, and social events so one can grow as both a programmer and
a person. Our industry connections, destressing games, and educational
workshops serve your success.

"Stony Brook Computing Society", as a registered organization at Stony
Brook University, is committed to promoting a safe and vibrant campus
community. The leaders and members of "The Stony Brook Computing
Society" also agree to abide by the Stony Brook University Code of
Student Responsibility, University policies, and Federal, New York
State, and Local laws.

Therefore, the rules, regulations, and policies of Stony Brook
University shall supersede any terms or processes outlined in this
constitution.

# Article III -- Membership 

## Section I

a.  The focus of the Stony Brook Computing Society (SBCS) shall be to
    > serve all student populations with a primary focus on
    > undergraduate students. Active membership must be limited to
    > currently enrolled students at Stony Brook University (SBU). The
    > Stony Brook Computing Society is open to all students,
    > irrespective of their fields of study, fostering a community that
    > thrives on diversity and a shared interest in technology.

b.  No person shall be denied membership or office because of sexual
    > orientation, nation of origin, race, sex, physical disability,
    > political or religious beliefs, and all other protected classes
    > under federal and state laws.

c.  Non-University members may participate, but may not act on behalf of
    > the organization or perform any of the following functions:
    > Holding office in the organization; Presiding, officiating,
    > voting, or making/seconding motions at any meeting of the
    > organization; Soliciting funds on campus on behalf of the
    > organization; Working at tables/events on the campus on behalf of
    > the organization unless a student member or University
    > faculty/staff is at all times present and responsible for the
    > activity.

## Section II

Active members are classified as members who have attended at least 3
events, GBMs, or SBCS events in the last year or this current semester.

# Article IV -- Executive Board Positions

## Section I: General Requirements

a.  The executive board is defined as the President, Vice President,
    > Treasurer, and Secretary. These executive board members must:

    i.  Hold only one position on the board unless otherwise approved by
        > SEA in writing

    ii. Be matriculated, currently enrolled, and working toward an
        > undergraduate degree relevant to the organization they
        > represent unless otherwise approved by SEA in writing.

    iii. Be in good standing with the University. Sanctions of
         > University Probation or higher, or a sanction restricting
         > participation in clubs/organizations or from holding
         > leadership positions, disqualify students from serving in an
         > executive board or leadership position.

    iv. Maintain at least a 2.25 cumulative GPA.

b.  All officers shall serve for 1 academic year and may be re-elected
    > at the end of their term, provided they maintain the requirements
    > noted above.

c.  All officers must serve as a mentor for SBCS's mentoring program,
    > Project Quack

## Section II: Organization

a.  The total membership of the executive board shall not exceed 50% of
    > the members of the organization.

## Section III: President

a.  The President shall be the official representative of the
    > organization; he/she shall also be a non-voting, ex-officio member
    > of all committees of the organization. The President shall be the
    > chairperson and presiding officer of the executive board.

b.  The President shall have the power to convene and preside over the
    > meetings of the executive board and the general body, as well as
    > prepare the agenda for the respective meetings.

c.  The President shall work together with the E-Board to delegate the
    > work of the E-Board.

d.  The President will be in charge of updating our advisors about our
    > club and moderating communications with sponsors and external
    > companies.

e.  The President shall oversee the Special Interest Group board members

f.  The President shall further perform such duties as may be necessary
    > and proper in the fulfillment of their office, subject to the
    > approval of the executive board.

## Section IV: Vice President

a.  The Vice President shall be required to see that standing committee
    > chairpersons are fulfilling their duties.

b.  The Vice President shall work with other clubs and organizations on
    > campus, including, but not limited to, the Career Center and the
    > CS Department, to connect SBCS and its members to other events
    > that can be of benefit to members.

c.  The Vice President shall further perform such duties as may be
    > necessary and proper in the fulfillment of his/her office, subject
    > to the approval of the executive board.

d.  If the President is unable to perform his/her duties (due to
    > impeachment, leave of absence, or resignation), the Vice President
    > shall assume the position of the President.

e.  If the Vice President is unable to perform their duties (due to
    > leave of absence, resignation, or impeachment), a new Vice
    > President will be elected in a special election called by the
    > President.

## Section V: Treasurer

a.  The Treasurer shall keep a detailed record of all monies allotted to
    > the organization. The Treasurer shall also submit all payment
    > requests.

b.  The Treasurer shall perform such duties as may be necessary and
    > proper in the fulfillment of his/her office, subject to the
    > approval of the executive board.

c.  If the Treasurer is unable to perform their duties (due to leave of
    > absence, resignation, or impeachment), a new Treasurer will be
    > elected in a special election called by the President.

d.  The Event Coordinator reports to the Treasurer.

## Section VI: Secretary

a.  The Secretary shall take minutes and attendance of all meetings and
    > maintain an up-to-date active voting membership roster.

b.  The Secretary shall send out a weekly email, which outlines the
    > agenda of the upcoming meeting, one school day before the meeting.

c.  If the Secretary is unable to perform their duties (due to leave of
    > absence, resignation, or impeachment), a new Secretary shall be
    > elected in a special election called by the President

d.  The Webmaster reports to the Secretary.

# Article V -- Non-Executive Board Positions

## Section I: General Requirements

a.  Non-executive board positions include the Branding Manager, Social
    > Media Manager, Event Coordinator, Web Coordinator, Project Quack
    > Director, and QuackHacks Director.

b.  Non-executive board positions must report to one of the executive
    > board members, as identified in the executive board members\'
    > responsibilities.

c.  Members holding a non-executive board position hold specific
    > responsibilities with the organization, however, they are not a
    > part of the executive board.

d.  Members holding a non-executive board position must be matriculated,
    > currently enrolled, and working toward an undergraduate degree
    > unless otherwise approved by SEA in writing.

e.  All non-executive board positions shall serve for 1 academic year.

f.  All non-executive board positions must serve as a mentor for SBCS's
    > mentoring program, Project Quack.

## Section II: Branding Manager

a.  The Branding Manager shall be in charge of creating flyers for SBCS
    > events and GBMs and coordinating flyer postings around campus.

b.  If the Branding Manager is unable to perform their duties (due to
    > leave of absence, resignation, or assumption of the presidency), a
    > new Branding Manager will be elected in a special election called
    > by the President.

c.  The Branding Manager reports to the Vice President of this
    > organization

## Section III: Social Media Manager

a.  The Social Media Manager shall be responsible for creating and
    > posting promotional content (posts and reels) on Instagram,
    > posting event announcements on Discord, and posting professional
    > club updates on LinkedIn

b.  If the Social Media Manager is unable to perform their duties (due
    > to leave of absence, resignation, or assumption of the
    > presidency), a new Community Chair will be elected in a special
    > election called by the President.

c.  The Social Media Manager reports to the Vice President of this
    > organization

## Section IV: Event Coordinator

a.  The responsibilities of an Event coordinator include coordinating
    > and planning SBCS events and collaborations, booking rooms on
    > 25Live, submitting events on SBEngaged, and staying in contact
    > with the club\'s program advisor from the Department of Student

b.  If the Event Coordinator is unable to perform their duties (due to
    > leave of absence, resignation, or assumption of the presidency), a
    > new Event Coordinator will be elected in a special election called
    > by the President.

c.  The Event Coordinator reports to the Treasurer of this organization

## Section V: Web Coordinator

a.  The responsibilities of the Web Coordinator include creating,
    > maintaining, and designing the SBCS website
    > ([[https://sbcs.io/]{.underline}](https://sbcs.io/)), as well as
    > any websites needed for SBCS events.

b.  If a Web Coordinator is unable to perform their duties (due to leave
    > of absence, resignation, or assumption of the presidency), a new
    > Web Coordinator will be elected in a special election called by
    > the President.

c.  The Web Coordinator reports to the Secretary of this organization

## Section VI: Project Quack Director

a.  The responsibilities of the Project Quack Director shall include

b.  If a Project Quack Director is unable to perform their duties (due

## Section VII: QuackHacks Director

a.  The responsibilities of the QuackHacks Director include being the
    > main point of contact for QuackHacks and Geek Games

        > the event

b.  If a QuackHacks Director is unable to perform their duties (due to

    > called by the President.

## Section VIII: Reps

a.  Reps, short for representatives, are extended e-board positions that
    > are elected through an interview process in the early fall of the
    > academic year

b.  The responsibilities of a representative include assisting with
    > event planning and hosting, which can involve coming up with event
    > ideas, designing activities, and leading workshops.

c.  If a Representative is unable to perform their duties (due to leave
    > of absence, resignation, or assumption of the presidency), a new
    > Representative will be elected in another interview process called
    > by the President.

# Article VI -- Special Interest Groups

## Section I: General Requirements

-   Non-executive board positions include Special Interest Group board
    > members

-   Special Interest Group board members must report to the President of
    > this organization.

-   Members holding a non-executive board position hold specific
    > responsibilities with the organization, however, they are not a
    > part of the executive board.

-   Members holding a non-executive board position must be matriculated,
    > currently enrolled, and working toward an undergraduate degree
    > unless otherwise approved by SEA in writing.

-   All non-executive board positions shall serve for one academic year.

## Section II: Responsibilities 

-   Special Interest Groups may be formed by members of this
    > organization to serve as forums for discussion of appropriate
    > specific computing interests

-   Special Interest Groups can be created/determined based on the needs
    > of the organization

-   Special Interest Groups are sub-committees of this organization and
    > will be named in the form "Computing Society X at Stony Brook,"
    > where X appropriately reflects the Special Interest Group's
    > purpose. This name shall be used for all business of the Special
    > Interest Group.

.

## Section III: Relationship with the Executive Board

-   The Executive Board must support all Special Interest Groups equally
    > in their endeavors by helping them secure meeting rooms, obtain
    > necessary funding from the SBCS budget for activities as deemed
    > appropriate by the Executive Board, and make their events and
    > activities known to the General Body.

-   Special Interest Groups may request funding from this organization's
    > budget, and receive organizational assistance from this
    > organization's members and executive board.

-   Special Interest Groups' requests for funding must be approved by
    > 3⁄4 of the Executive Board, and can then be added to this
    > organization's budgetary request following official CEAS or SBLife
    > policies.

## Section IV: Structure of Special Interest Groups 

a.  Special Interest Groups shall elect an Executive Board of at least a
    > President and Vice President to plan their meetings and activities
    > and report to the General Body. Members of SBCS's Executive Board
    > may not be on any Special Interest Group's Executive Board, though
    > they can be active members of any Special Interest Group, as they
    > must advise and assist all Special Interest Groups equally.

b.  To keep their status, Special Interest Groups must have at least 3
    > activities or meetings per semester and must send an informed
    > representative (such as an Executive Board member) to each General
    > Body Meeting of this organization to update the General Body
    > members on their events and activities.

c.  If a Special Interest Group fails to elect a full Executive Board,
    > hold three (3) meetings or activities, including joint activities
    > with this organization, other SIGs, or other organizations, or
    > fails to send a representative to General Body meetings five (5)
    > times during a semester, it shall be considered defunct and may
    > not operate or receive funding until it is formally re-formed.

## Section V: Creation of Special Interest Groups

a.  A Special Interest Group can be formed at any time during the
    > semester, and request funding at the beginning of the next
    > semester after it was formed in time for the official organization
    > budget to be made.

b.  To be formed, Special Interest Groups must be proposed by an
    > interested student and approved by three-quarters (3/4) of the
    > Executive Board.

c.  To be voted upon, a Special Interest Group must have a name, a brief
    > statement of purpose, and a proposed Executive Board (if a SIG is
    > proposed at any other time than at the beginning of a semester,
    > its Executive Board shall continue through the next semester).

# Article VII -- Meetings

a.  This organization shall hold a General Body Meeting weekly if
    > possible, and at least one General Body meeting each month during
    > the Fall or Spring semester is in session.

b.  A quorum consisting of a majority of the total active voting
    > membership is necessary for the transaction of any business at all
    > meetings.

c.  All meetings must have an agenda and five (5) school days prior
    > notice of the meetings must be given to all organization members.

# Article VIII -- Elections

## Section I: Executive Board Selection

a.  Officers of the Executive Board shall be determined by an election
    > with the active body that is eligible to vote.

b.  No person shall seek a position on the executive board unless they
    > have been an active member of this organization for at least one
    > semester before the semester when they would assume office.

c.  Nomination Process

    i.  Nominees for office must fulfill the general requirements for
        > being elected/appointed to office.

    ii. Nominations must be submitted/take place a minimum of one week
        > prior to the selection of officers.

    iii. Nominations can be made by either

         1.  Executive Board Member at a general body meeting

         2.  General Body Members at a general body meeting

    iv. Self Nominations are permitted

    v.  All nominees will be allowed to accept or decline the
        > nomination(s)

d.  Election Process

No person shall be elected to more than one executive board position at
any given term.

i.  An election is won by a simple majority vote (50% +1) of the active
    > members present at the election. A quorum is required.

ii. Officers shall be elected in the following order President, Vice
    > President, Secretary, Treasurer

iii. Nominees shall be called in alphabetical order to the order of the
     > position, all other nominees shall leave the room until called in
     > by the President or their designee

iv. Nominees shall be able to speak about their qualifications and
    > desires for the position for a maximum of two minutes.

v.  After all nominees for the positions have spoken about their
    > qualifications and character for the position, active members may
    > ask questions to the nominees to help solidify their vote for the
    > position.

vi. Elections must be held no later than April.

vii. Elections shall be conducted through anonymous ballot, done
     > virtually through the SBEngaged Elections Tab or in person.

viii. In the event a simple majority is not reached, the top two
      > candidates shall be allowed to speak for 1 additional minute and
      > a new ballot shall be collected.

ix. Once all positions have been filled through the completion of the
    > Election Process, the change shall be recorded in the minutes, and
    > submitted on SBEngaged through the Executive Board Update form.

# Article IX -- Impeachment of Executive Board Officers

## Section I: Grounds for Removal

a.  Any officer may be brought up on impeachment charges if they have
    failed to uphold the duties of the officer as set forth by this
    constitution.

b.  Any executive and non-executive board member who misses **two or
    more** weekly executive board meetings in an academic year unexcused
    (as defined below) will be subject to a vote to remain in their
    positions

    a.  An excused absence includes, but is not limited to 1) a midterm
        > anytime between the time of the executive board meeting and
        > the end of the following day, 2) sickness, 3) other
        > emergencies

    b.  A virtual option will be available in these circumstances if the
        > board member is not able to be physically present, e.g. the
        > member happens to be traveling during an executive board
        > meeting. The virtual option will not be available to any board
        > member who is deemed able to physically attend the meeting.

## Section II: Procedure for Impeachment

a.  A list of charges and supplemental materials shall be presented to
    an executive board member by any active member of the organization.

b.  Within one week of validation of charges as outlined in Section I,
    an impeachment proceeding must be held, and presided by the
    President. If the President is being brought up on charges, the Vice
    President shall be the presiding officer.

    i.  The organization must immediately notify Student Engagement &
        > Activities once validation of charges has occurred.

c.  The impeachment proceedings shall occur when classes are in session,
    or as soon as possible if classes end within one week after the
    validation of charges have occurred.

d.  An officer must be informed of charges against them in writing
    within one week of the validation of charges.

e.  If the charged officer does not attend the impeachment proceedings,
    one rescheduling of the proceedings shall be allowed before the
    officer in question is automatically removed from the Executive
    Board.

f.  Witnesses who have submitted a list of charges shall have the
    opportunity to present their evidence in favor of the removal of the
    charged officer during the impeachment proceedings.

g.  The officer who has been charged shall have the opportunity to
    present any counter-evidence and present a defense during the
    impeachment proceedings.

h.  After each side has presented their case, a majority vote (51%) of
    the current members in office, including the executive and
    non-executive board, but excluding the reps and the officer being
    brought up on charges, present is needed for the charged officer to
    be removed from office.

i.  If the officer is removed from office, written correspondence to the
    removed officer, Program Advisor and Faculty/Staff advisor (if
    applicable) should be sent within a business day of their removal.
    All written correspondence about the officer\'s impeachment should
    be stored in appropriate files for record-keeping purposes.

## Section III: Appeal of Impeachment

a.  After a charged officer has been removed from office, they shall
    have seven (7) calendar days to appeal the removal.

b.  The appeal must be submitted in writing to the executive board and
    must include any relevant information that has not already been
    presented. The Acting President will then submit the appeal to the
    Appeals Committee.

c.  The Appeals Committee shall consist of the executive and
    non-executive board members.

d.  This committee shall then render a decision at the next general body
    meeting or within 7 calendar days, whichever occurs first.

## Section IV: Special Elections for Replacement of Removed Officers

a.  In the event of the President's removal from office, the Vice
    President shall assume the powers and responsibilities of the
    Presidency until a special election is held for a new President
    within one month.

b.  In the event of the removal of an officer other than the President,
    a special election shall be held within one month.

c.  The President shall be responsible for the roles and
    responsibilities of the vacant position until a special election is
    held.

d.  Special elections shall be held following the election process
    outlined in Article VII.

# Article X: Removal of Non-Executive Board Positions

## Section I: Grounds for Removal

a.  Any non-executive board member may be removed from their position if
    they have failed to uphold the duties of the position as set forth
    by this constitution.

## Section II: Procedure for Removal

a.  Appointed positions by the executive board may be removed at any
    point at the discretion of the executive board officer, so long as
    the appointee(s) have been given proper notice and opportunity for
    corrective action

    i.  Proper notice includes a written warning to the position holder
        > outlining the failure to uphold their duties, a plan outlining
        > the corrective action the position holder shall take, and a
        > timeline for them to take said corrective action. If
        > corrective action is not taken by the designated timeline, the
        > position holder shall be notified in writing of their removal
        > from the position.

    ii. Removal will occur upon a ¾ majority vote from the executive
        > board

## Section III: Appeal of Removal

a.  When a non-executive board position is removed from office, written
    correspondence to the removed officer, Program Advisor, and
    Faculty/Staff advisor (if applicable) should be sent within a
    business day of their removal. All written correspondence about the
    position holder's impeachment should be stored in appropriate files
    for record-keeping purposes.

b.  After a non-executive board member has been removed from office,
    they shall have seven (7) calendar days to appeal the removal.

c.  The appeal must be submitted in writing to the executive board and
    must include any relevant information that has not already been
    presented. The Acting President will then submit the appeal to the
    Appeals Committee.

d.  The Appeals Committee shall consist of the executive board members

e.  This committee shall then render a decision at a closed meeting or
    within 7 calendar days, whichever occurs first.

# Article XI -- Amendments

a.  Amendments to this constitution may be introduced by any current
    > officer, excluding the reps.

b.  The entire board shall be notified in writing at least five (5)
    > school days before voting on a proposed amendment. A majority vote
    > of all members in office, including executive board and
    > non-executive board but excluding reps, is necessary for the
    > passage of an amendment.

# Article XII -- Dissolution

In the event of the dissolution of this organization, all accrued funds
and assets given by the Undergraduate Student Government shall revert to
the Undergraduate Student Government, and all accrued funds that are
placed in an FSA or TNA account shall be used at the discretion of the
University-designated signatory.
